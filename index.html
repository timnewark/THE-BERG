Frequently Asked Questions

Your answers to our frequently asked questions about uPVC windows and doors, covering installation, efficiency, security, durability and aftercare.

Double glazing offers several advantages over single glazing. By trapping an insulating layer of air or gas between two panes of glass, it significantly improves energy efficiency by reducing heat loss and keeping warmth inside your home.

This insulating barrier also helps to reduce external noise, creating a quieter indoor environment.
Beyond comfort and noise reduction, double glazing can help lower energy bills and may add value to your property, making it a smart long-term investment.

Condensation can sometimes form between the panes of a double-glazed window. This usually happens when the sealed unit has failed, allowing warm air from inside the home to enter the gap between the glass. When this warm air meets the cooler surface of the window, condensation can develop inside the unit.

If you notice condensation trapped between the panes, it’s often a sign that the glazing has reached the end of its lifespan and may need replacing or upgrading.

At the-berg, our windows are manufactured and installed to high standards to help prevent failed seals, draughts and moisture build-up between panes. If you’re experiencing issues with existing windows, we’re happy to provide advice and a no-obligation quote.

There isn’t a single fixed price for replacing windows, as costs vary depending on several factors. These include the size and style of your home, the type of window or door you choose, glazing specifications, colours and finishes and the complexity of the installation. Manufacturing quality and installation time can also influence the final cost.

Our experienced advisors can talk through your options and provide a clear, no-obligation quote based on your specific requirements. This ensures you receive accurate pricing with no surprises, along with guidance on the most suitable solutions for your home.

We offer a wide range of replacement windows to suit different property styles and requirements. Options include sash windows for traditional and period homes, modern casement windows and tilt and turn windows that provide added flexibility for ventilation and easy cleaning.

Whichever style you choose, all our windows are supplied and installed to high standards and are backed by long-term guarantees, giving you confidence in their performance, durability and quality.

The biggest improvement in energy efficiency is typically achieved when upgrading from single glazing to modern ‘A’ rated double glazing. While there can be further gains when moving from double to triple glazing, these are often incremental and may not always justify the additional cost.

In our experience, double glazing offers the best balance between affordability and performance, providing excellent thermal insulation, reduced noise levels and strong energy ratings. Triple glazing is usually only recommended in specific situations, such as properties located near very busy roads where enhanced sound reduction may be beneficial.
